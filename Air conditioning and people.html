Main usage scenarios of air conditioners

Air conditioners are widely used across various settings, encompassing residential, office, and industrial environments. Here are some detailed use cases:

  • Residential Use: In homes, air conditioners are used to maintain a comfortable indoor temperature and humidity, especially during summer and winter, to improve living comfort.
  • Offices and Commercial Spaces: Air conditioners are extensively used in offices, shopping malls, restaurants, and other commercial spaces to ensure a conducive work and shopping environment.
  • Industrial Applications: In industrial settings, air conditioners are used to maintain specific temperature and humidity conditions, which are crucial for the normal operation of certain precision manufacturing processes and equipment.
  • Medical and Laboratory Facilities: In hospitals' operating rooms, laboratories, and pharmaceutical factories, air conditioners are used to control cleanliness and temperature and humidity to ensure the safety and precision of medical operations.
  • Data Centers: Air conditioners in data centers are used to control temperatures to prevent electronic equipment from overheating, ensuring stable system operation.
  • Transportation: In airplanes, trains, cars, and other modes of transportation, air conditioners are used to provide a comfortable travel environment for passengers.
  • Hotels and Entertainment Venues: In hotels, cinemas, and stadiums, air conditioners are used to create a comfortable and pleasant environment.
  • Educational Institutions: In schools and libraries, air conditioners help create an environment conducive to learning and research.

Advantages and Disadvantages of Air Conditioning

Advantage:

  • High Comfort: Air conditioners can quickly adjust indoor temperatures to provide a comfortable living and working environment.
  • Energy Efficiency:Modern air conditioners often come with energy-saving technologies, such as inverters, which can effectively reduce energy consumption.
  • Multifunctional: Many air conditioners have additional features like dehumidification and air purification, improving indoor air quality.
  • Aesthetically Pleasing: Central air conditioning systems are hidden installations that coordinate with interior design styles without occupying extra space.
  • Reduce humidity: In humid climates, air conditioners can reduce indoor humidity and prevent mold growth

Disadvantages:

  • High Installation Cost: Especially for central air conditioning systems, the initial investment is quite high.
  • Maintenance Cost: Air conditioners require regular cleaning and maintenance, or they may affect performance and lifespan.
  • Potential Health Issues: If not properly set or maintained, air conditioning can lead to respiratory problems or other health issues.
  • Environmental Impact: Air conditioners use refrigerants that can have a negative impact on the environment.

Impact of air conditioning on the environment

  • Greenhouse Effect: The refrigerants used in air conditioners, especially chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs), release greenhouse gases that contribute to global warming.
  • Ozone Layer Depletion: Certain types of refrigerants can destroy the ozone layer in the atmosphere, leading to increased ultraviolet radiation.
  • Urban Heat Island Effect: The heat emitted by the external units of air conditioners can raise the surrounding temperatures, which may exacerbate the urban heat island effect, especially in cities.
  • Energy Consumption:Air conditioners require a significant amount of electricity to operate, and the production of electricity can result in the emission of carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases.
  • Noise Pollution:The external units of air conditioners can generate noise during operation, causing disturbance to nearby residents.
  • Water Pollution: Improperly treated condensate water from air conditioners can lead to water pollution.
  • Resource Consumption: The production and transportation of air conditioners consume a significant amount of raw materials and energy, which could lead to resource depletion.
  • Waste Disposal: The dismantling and disposal of old air conditioning systems can generate a considerable amount of waste, which, if not properly managed, can pollute the environment.

Home air conditioning systems everywhere

Home air conditioning systems are most common and popular in West Asia. Due to the hot weather and high living standards in summer, air conditioning has become a daily necessity. With the improvement of living standards, air conditioning has gradually become popular in wealthy communities in Southeast Asia with tropical climates, such as Malaysia, Vietnam and the Philippines.

East Asia, such as Japan, Mainland China, Hong Kong, Singapore and Taiwan, are important producing areas for air conditioners. Most air conditioning systems manufactured in this region are window type (window type) or split type, with the split type being more advanced and expensive.

In the United States, home air conditioning systems are more common in the east coast and south. However, in the north and west coast of the United States, summer temperatures are not high, so people rarely use them. However, central air conditioning systems are more common in the United States and have become the unofficial standard for new homes in Florida. In countries in the southern hemisphere such as South America and Australia, the summer is also very hot in December, so northern areas are also equipped with air conditioners.

In Europe, home air conditioning systems are relatively rare, partly because Europe has a milder climate without hot summers and social reasons. For example, Spanish people traditionally perform siesta and travel north to France to spend long summer vacations; but the world's climate The phenomenon of warming may make air conditioning systems more common. Lack of air conditioning in homes, nursing homes and medical facilities may have contributed to the 2003 heat wave in Europe that killed 35,000 people.

The history of the invention of air conditioning

Passive cooling wind towers have long appeared in North Africa, West Asia and India.

In the 19th century, the British scientist and inventor Michael Faraday discovered that compressing and liquefying a certain gas could freeze the air. This phenomenon occurred when liquefied ammonia evaporated. His idea was still theoretical at the time.

In 1842, Florida physician John B. Gorey used compressor technology to create ice to provide cool air to patients at his hospital in Apalachicola, Florida. He wanted to use this ice-making technology to regulate the temperature of the building and applied for a patent. However, due to lack of funds, it was not implemented. Alfred R Wolff (1859-1909), an engineer in Hoboken, New Jersey, helped design a new air-conditioning system and applied the technology to commercial buildings. He is considered one of the pioneers of air-conditioning. One, but not famous.

In late 1902, the first modern, electrically powered air conditioning system was invented by Willis Carrier. The difference between its design and Wolf's design is that it not only controls the temperature, but also controls the humidity of the air to improve the process quality of a printing factory in Brooklyn, New York. This technology provides a low temperature and humidity environment, making the paper area and ink arrangement more accurate. That is why the dehumidifier was invented. Later, Carrier's technology began to be used in the workplace to improve production efficiency.

With gradual development, air conditioning began to be used to improve the comfort of homes and cars. Built in 1906, the Royal Victoria Hospital in Belfast, Northern Ireland, is of special significance in terms of architectural engineering and is known as the world's first air-conditioned building.

In 1906, Stuart W. Cramer of Charlotte, North Carolina, USA, was looking for ways to increase the air humidity in his southern textile mills. Cramer named the technology "air conditioning" and patented it in the same year as a replacement for water conditioning. Water conditioning was a well-known procedure at the time that made textile production easier. He combined water vapor with ventilation systems to "condition" and transform the air in the factory, controlling air humidity, which is all-important in textile factories. Willis Carrier used this name and put it into the name of the company he founded in 1907: "American Gallia Air Conditioning Company" (today's Carrier Company).

The original air conditioners and refrigerators used toxic gases such as ammonia and methyl chloride. The leakage of this type of gas can cause major accidents. Thomas Midgley invented chlorofluorocarbon gas in 1928 and named it Freon. This refrigerant is much safer for humans, but is harmful to the atmospheric ozone layer. Freon is DuPont's trademark for CFC, HCFC or HFC refrigerants, each of which also includes a number to indicate the molecular makeup of its ingredients (e.g. R-11, R-12, R-22, R- 134). Among them, R-22 HCFC refrigerant, which is the most widely used in the field of direct evaporative moderate cooling products, has been discontinued in newly produced equipment since 2010, and will be completely discontinued in 2020. R-11 and R-12 have been discontinued in the United States. As an alternative, some ozone-friendly refrigerants have been put into use, including the refrigerant R-410A under the trade name "Puron".

The spread of air conditioning

The development and popularization of air conditioning has gone through several important stages:

  1. The birth of electric air conditioning in the United States: In 1902, Willis Carrier invented the prototype of an early electric air conditioner, which was mainly used to provide a suitable working environment for industry.
  2. Promotion of air conditioning in the United States:In 1924, air conditioning began to be popularized in public places such as shopping malls, and was later widely installed in movie theaters and other entertainment venues after World War II.
  3. The birth and differentiated development of air conditioners in Japan and South Korea: In the 1950s, Carrier air conditioners entered the Japanese and Korean markets, promoting the development of local air conditioning technology.
  4. The rise of air conditioning in China:In 1937, the Bank of China Building began to use air conditioning. After 1993, with the entry of foreign air-conditioning companies, China's air-conditioning market developed rapidly.

The advancement of air conditioning technology, especially the development of energy-saving technology and the reduction of production costs, have gradually made air conditioning a standard feature for ordinary households. Nowadays, air conditioning is not only a savior in summer, but also an important part of smart home systems, and its development is still ongoing.
